The key is the sealer: stamped driveways must be resealed every 3–5 years to protect against de-icing salts and UV fade. Avoid using rock salt on stamped concrete — use sand or a low-salt ice melt instead.
Stamped concrete is typically 20–40% less expensive than quality concrete pavers or natural stone. It's a monolithic slab, so no individual pieces shift or settle, and no weeds grow between joints. The downside: repairs to a specific area are harder to match seamlessly. Both options outlast asphalt by decades when properly installed.
For driveways, we recommend patterns with texture that provide traction when wet: slate, Ashlar, cobblestone, or running bond brick all work well. Smooth stone patterns with minimal texture can be slippery when icy — we add anti-slip additive to the sealer on any smooth-pattern driveway as a standard practice.
