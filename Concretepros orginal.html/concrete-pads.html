Concrete pads seem simple, but the thickness and reinforcement requirements vary significantly by intended use. An AC condenser pad can be 3.5 inches over minimal base — it just needs to stay level and drain. A hot tub base needs 5–6 inches of concrete with proper reinforcement (rebar or fiber) because a filled hot tub weighs 3,000–5,000 lbs. A sport court needs control joints placed correctly to allow for thermal expansion. We size every pad correctly for its load requirements — not just the cheapest pour that gets the job done short-term.

FAQ — Concrete Pads & Slabs

A standard shed pad should be 4 inches thick on 4 inches of compacted gravel base — sufficient for a storage shed up to approximately 16×20 feet. For a larger shed or one that will store heavy equipment like tractors or ATVs, we recommend 5–6 inches with fiber or rebar reinforcement.
A standard AC condenser pad (3×3 to 4×4 feet) typically costs $350–$650 installed including base prep, forming, pour, and finish. We can also replace existing AC pads that have settled or cracked. Some HVAC installers require a level concrete pad as part of their installation — we can coordinate timing for new equipment installations.
Permit requirements for concrete pads vary by municipality and pad size. Smaller AC pads and shed pads under a certain square footage are often exempt. Larger pads, additions to driveways, or pads under buildings typically require permits. We determine permit requirements during your free estimate.
