2026 Concrete Driveway Costs in McHenry County, IL

Updated 2026 pricing for concrete driveway replacement and stamped driveways across Crystal Lake, Woodstock, Cary, Algonquin, and Lake in the Hills, Illinois.

What Drives Concrete Driveway Costs in 2026

Concrete driveway pricing in McHenry County depends on several variables: the size of the driveway, demolition requirements, the chosen finish, site accessibility, and current material costs. In 2026, concrete material prices have stabilized after several years of volatility, but labor costs continue to reflect a competitive skilled trades market in the Chicagoland area.

Standard Broom-Finish Driveway

A standard 2-car broom-finish concrete driveway replacement in McHenry County runs approximately $4,500–$8,000 for a typical 400–600 square foot slab. This includes demolition and haul-away of existing concrete, proper base prep with compacted gravel, a 4-inch reinforced slab, standard broom texture, and control joints.

3-Car and Wider Driveways

Wider driveways — 3-car configurations or long single-lane driveways to detached garages — typically run $7,000–$14,000 for 600–1,000 square feet. The price per square foot often decreases slightly at larger sizes due to efficiencies in the pour and finishing process.

Important: Beware of extremely low bids. Bids significantly below market rate often indicate shortcuts — thin slabs, inadequate base prep, no reinforcement, or substandard concrete mixes. These save money upfront but fail early in Illinois winters.

Stamped Concrete Driveways

Stamped concrete driveways command a premium due to the additional labor, materials, and skill required. Expect $14–$22 per square foot for stamped driveways with a single color and standard patterns. Multi-color work with borders or custom patterns can run $20–$30 per square foot.

What's Included in a Proper Installation

  • Demolition and haul-away of existing concrete
  • Excavation and grading to proper depth
  • Compacted aggregate base (6–8 inches minimum)
  • 4-inch reinforced concrete slab (6-inch for heavy vehicles)
  • Control joints placed at appropriate intervals
  • Curing compound for proper strength development
  • Permit coordination if required by your municipality
