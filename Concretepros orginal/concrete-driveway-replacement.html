A concrete driveway replacement starts with full demolition of your existing slab and haul-out of all debris. Then comes the most critical part that separates long-lasting driveways from failures: base preparation. We excavate to proper depth and install 4–6 inches of compacted crushed aggregate, then form, reinforce, and pour with air-entrained concrete mix rated for Illinois freeze-thaw conditions. Control joints are placed at correct intervals to manage cracking. The finish is applied, curing compound is sprayed, and the forms come off 24–48 hours later.

FAQ — Concrete Driveway Replacement

Most standard driveway replacements in McHenry County take 1–2 days for the actual work. Add 3–10 business days for permit approval depending on your municipality. You can park on the new concrete after 7 days.
Patching is cost-effective for isolated cracks and minor spalling, but it's typically a temporary fix. If your driveway has widespread cracking, heaving, or is crumbling at the edges, replacement is almost always the better investment over the long term.
We use air-entrained 4,000 PSI concrete — the standard for Illinois climates with hard freeze-thaw cycles. This mix contains microscopic air bubbles that allow water to expand and contract within the slab without cracking. We pour at 4 inches minimum thickness over properly compacted base.
