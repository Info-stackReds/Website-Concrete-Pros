Sitting walls serve multiple purposes: they define outdoor spaces, create seating areas around patios, manage grade transitions, and add strong architectural interest to any landscape. We build sitting walls from concrete block, natural stone veneer over concrete block, or poured concrete — each with its own aesthetic and price point. Retaining walls require proper drainage behind the wall (crushed stone backfill and drainage pipe) to prevent hydrostatic pressure buildup that causes walls to lean or fail. Walls over 4 feet typically require engineering sign-off depending on your municipality.

FAQ — Sitting & Retaining Walls

Most McHenry County municipalities require permits for retaining walls over a certain height — typically 3–4 feet. Crystal Lake, Woodstock, Algonquin, and Cary all have permit requirements for larger retaining walls. We determine permit requirements as part of your free estimate.
A sitting wall is primarily decorative and functional seating — typically 18–30 inches tall, built on a concrete footing. A retaining wall holds back a slope or grade change and must be engineered to handle the lateral soil pressure behind it. Retaining walls require proper drainage to function long-term.
Properly constructed concrete block walls in Illinois climates last 25–50 years or more. The key to longevity is proper foundation depth below frost line (42 inches in McHenry County) and adequate drainage behind retaining walls to prevent freeze-thaw pressure buildup.
