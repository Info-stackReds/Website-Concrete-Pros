Every concrete patio we install includes proper grade assessment for drainage, minimum 4-inch concrete thickness, and a compacted gravel base. We offer broom finish (most popular and durable), smooth trowel finish, exposed aggregate, and stamped decorative concrete. All patios are formed with proper slope away from the home — typically 1/8 inch per foot — and control joints cut at appropriate intervals. We apply curing compound immediately after finishing to protect against too-rapid moisture loss during the critical first 24 hours.

FAQ — Concrete Patio Installation

Residential patios should be poured at a minimum of 4 inches thick on a properly prepared base. If you plan to park a vehicle on the patio or it connects to a driveway, we recommend 5–6 inch thickness. Patio thickness is one of the most common areas where contractors cut costs — always ask what thickness is included in any quote you receive.
You can walk on new concrete after 24–48 hours, and place patio furniture after 7 days. For heavy outdoor appliances or grills on wheels, wait 10–14 days. Full concrete cure is 28 days — the surface continues to harden throughout this period.
Permit requirements vary by municipality. Many McHenry County cities require permits for patios above a certain size (often 150–200 sq ft). We determine permit requirements as part of your free estimate and handle all applications.
